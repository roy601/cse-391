Personal

Hobbies & Interests

Outside of code and research, here’s what keeps me curious and grounded.

☕

Competitive Programming

I enjoy solving algorithmic problems on Codeforces in my spare time. It keeps my problem-solving instincts sharp and teaches me to think about efficiency before I even open a code editor.

♟

Chess

Chess is the closest thing to pure strategy I know. I play casually online and find that the positional thinking carries over into software architecture more than I expected.

Exploring Cities

There’s something compelling about walking through a city you don’t know. I’m fascinated by how urban spaces reflect the culture and constraints of the people who built them.

🎧

Music While Coding

Lo-fi, classical guitar, and the occasional film score. I firmly believe the right playlist is a legitimate productivity tool — not just background noise.

📖

Reading

Mostly CS classics (SICP, The Pragmatic Programmer) and the occasional piece on economics or history. I like books that change how I think, not just what I know.

🌶

Building Side Projects

Half my hobbies turn into side projects. I prototype ideas quickly to validate whether they’re worth building — most aren’t, and that’s fine. The process itself is the point.
